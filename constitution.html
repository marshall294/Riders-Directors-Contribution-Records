THE CONSTITUTION OF RIDERS FOOTBALL CLUB

Established for Unity • Discipline • Excellence

PREAMBLE

We, the members of Riders Football Club, a not-for-profit and non-political organisation, do firmly and solemnly resolve to provide for ourselves a constitution and to be governed by the provisions therein contained.

ARTICLE 1: NAME

The name of the Association is Riders Football Club.

ARTICLE 2: ADDRESS

SULEIMAN BARAU TECHNICAL COLLEGE, KWAMBA 910101, SULEJA, NIGER STATE, NIGERIA

ARTICLE 3: AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

  • To inspire passion for football, foster sportsmanship, and empower individuals through teamwork and excellence.
  • To promote unity and collaboration both on and off the field.
  • To uphold respect for the game, officials, teammates, opponents, and supporters.
  • To strive for excellence in player development and community engagement.
  • To maintain an inclusive environment where players of all backgrounds can thrive.
  • To engage actively with the community and promote youth development and well-being.
  • To become a leading football club known for talent development and strong community impact.

ARTICLE 4: TRUSTEES

The Trustees shall be elected at a General Meeting by two-thirds majority vote of members present. Key conditions include:

  • Trustees shall be between 2 and 15 in number.
  • A Trustee may serve for 5 years but ceases to hold office if they resign, are removed, become unfit, or relocate outside Nigeria.
  • Vacancies shall be filled at a General Meeting.

ARTICLE 5: COMMON SEAL

  • The Club shall have a Common Seal.
  • The Secretary shall keep the seal and present it when required.
  • Documents executed by Trustees must be signed and sealed using the Common Seal.

ARTICLE 6: MEETINGS

For effective administration, the club holds the following meetings:

  • Annual General Meeting: Quorum — 2/3 of members.
  • Special General Meeting: Quorum — 2/3 of members.
  • Monthly General Meeting: Quorum — 2/3 of members.
  • Executive Board Meeting: At least 2 Trustees.

ARTICLE 7: GOVERNING BODY

The Governing Body shall manage the day-to-day operations of the club. It includes:

  • Chairman
  • Vice Chairman
  • Secretary
  • Financial Secretary
  • Treasurer
  • Public Relations Officer
  • Welfare Officer
  • Legal Adviser
  • Board of Trustees

ARTICLE 8: SOURCES OF INCOME

  • Donations from individuals, Trustees, and organisations.
  • Grants and support from charitable bodies.
  • Private sector participation and contributions from NGOs and international agencies.

ARTICLE 9: DISBURSEMENT OF FUNDS

Funds shall be used strictly for achieving the aims and objectives of Riders FC.

ARTICLE 10: KEEPING ACCOUNTS

The Club shall maintain bank accounts with reputable banks. Signatories include:

  • The Chairman
  • The Treasurer
  • The Secretary

The Chairman and any one signatory may approve withdrawals.

ARTICLE 11: APPOINTMENT OF AUDITORS

  • Independent licensed auditors shall audit the financial records annually.
  • Audited financial statements shall be submitted to CAC.

ARTICLE 12: AMENDMENT OF CONSTITUTION

Amendments require a simple majority vote at a General Meeting and approval by the Corporate Affairs Commission.

ARTICLE 13: SPECIAL CLAUSE

All income and property of Riders FC shall be used solely for club objectives. No profit shall be distributed to members.

  • Officers may receive compensation only for services actually rendered.
  • Upon dissolution, remaining assets shall be transferred to a similar organisation.
