FINANCIAL REGULATIONS

1. Purpose of Financial Regulations

These financial regulations provide a clear and accountable structure for how funds of Riders FC are generated, managed, approved, disbursed, and audited. They ensure transparency, prevent misuse of funds, and promote financial discipline within the organisation.

2. Sources of Funds

Club funds may be generated through the following:

  • Monthly dues and general contributions from members.
  • Fines, fees, penalties, and administrative charges.
  • Fundraising events or activities organised by the club.
  • Voluntary donations, gifts, and sponsorships.
  • Any approved external support that aligns with the values of Riders FC.

3. Financial Custodians

The following officers are responsible for club finances:

  • The Treasurer — Primary custodian of all funds, maintains records and balances.
  • The Financial Secretary — Receives and records all payments before transferring to the Treasurer.
  • The President — Approves major financial decisions and authorises disbursement of funds.

4. Banking and Cash Handling

  • All club funds must be paid into a designated Riders FC account within 48 hours of collection.
  • No officer is allowed to keep club funds beyond reasonable processing time.
  • Withdrawals or transfers must be authorised by at least two signatories.

5. Disbursement of Funds

Payments can only be made for approved club activities. Requirements include:

  • A written request stating purpose and amount.
  • Approval by the President and either Treasurer or Secretary.
  • All expenditures must be receipted and recorded.

6. Financial Reporting

  • Monthly financial reports must be presented during the monthly meeting.
  • Records must include credits, debits, balance, and all supporting receipts.
  • Members have the right to request clarifications on financial matters.

7. Budgeting

The club shall prepare an annual financial plan or budget. Spending outside the budget requires emergency approval.

8. Auditing

  • An independent auditor or internal audit committee shall review the accounts yearly.
  • Audit reports must be presented to the general house for review and adoption.

9. Penalties for Mismanagement

Any officer found guilty of financial misconduct—including misappropriation, diversion, or fraud—shall face disciplinary actions which may include removal from office, refund of funds, suspension, or legal action.
