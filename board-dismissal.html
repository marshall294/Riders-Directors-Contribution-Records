BOARD MEMBER DISMISSAL POLICY

Riders Football Club

This policy outlines the correct, fair, and transparent procedure for dismissing a Board Member of Riders FC.

1. Purpose of This Policy

To ensure dismissals are not based on personal conflict, bias, or emotion, Riders FC establishes a clear and fair framework for removing a Board Member when necessary.

2. Grounds for Dismissal

A Board Member may be dismissed for any of the following reasons:

  • Financial Misconduct — Misappropriation, diversion, or misuse of club funds.
  • Gross Misconduct — Acts that tarnish the image of Riders FC, including assault, fraud, or unethical behaviour.
  • Neglect of Duties — Failure to perform assigned responsibilities over an extended period.
  • Absenteeism — Missing three (3) consecutive official meetings without valid reason.
  • Conflict of Interest — Acting in ways that benefit personal interest over the club’s interest.
  • Insubordination — Refusal to follow club rules, constitutional provisions, or official directives.
  • Criminal Conviction — Being found guilty of a criminal offence by a competent court.
  • Violation of the Club Constitution — Any serious breach of the constitution or official policies.

3. Complaint & Investigation Process

Dismissal must begin with a formal, documented process to prevent unfair treatment:

  • A complaint must be submitted in writing to the Secretary or Chairman.
  • The Executive Committee shall set up an Investigation Panel of 3–5 neutral members.
  • The accused Board Member must be notified in writing and given the opportunity to defend themselves.
  • The panel shall gather evidence, interview witnesses, and present a written report within 14 days.

4. Fair Hearing

Before any dismissal decision, the Board Member has the right to:

  • Respond to all allegations.
  • Provide witnesses or documents in their defence.
  • Be heard before the General House or Executive Committee.

5. Voting Process for Dismissal

Dismissal can only occur through a structured voting process:

  • A General Meeting must be convened.
  • Quorum: At least 2/3 majority of registered members must be present.
  • A minimum of 2/3 majority vote from members present is required for dismissal.
  • The vote may be done openly or through secret ballot depending on sensitivity.

6. Immediate Suspension

In cases of serious misconduct, the Executive Committee may suspend the Board Member pending full investigation. Suspension is not equal to dismissal.

7. Post-Dismissal Actions

  • The dismissed member loses all rights, privileges, and entitlements of their office.
  • They may only receive financial benefits earned before the dismissal date.
  • They must return all club property (documents, funds, kits, identification etc.).
  • A replacement may be appointed or elected following constitutional rules.

8. Appeals

The dismissed member may file an appeal within 14 days. The Appeals Committee shall review and either uphold or overturn the dismissal.

9. Protection Against Abuse of Power

  • No member shall be dismissed based on personal issues, tribalism, discrimination, or bias.
  • All decisions must align with the Club Constitution and Nigerian law.
