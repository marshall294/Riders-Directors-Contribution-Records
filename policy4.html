Riders Football Club

Governing Body & Executive Committee

Introduction

The Governing Body of Riders Football Club is responsible for the management, administration, and effective running of the club. These Officers ensure that all club activities align with the Constitution, vision, and objectives of Riders FC.

Composition of the Governing Body

According to the Constitution, the Governing Body / Executive Committee consists of all elected officers and any additional members elected by the club. They oversee planning, coordination, decision‑making, and enforcement of club policies.

1. Chairman

Provides leadership, presides over meetings, and ensures the smooth running of club affairs. Represents the club in official matters and oversees the execution of all policies and projects.

2. Vice Chairman

Assists the Chairman and acts in his stead when necessary. Helps manage internal affairs and ensures continuity of leadership.

3. Secretary

Oversees correspondence, keeps accurate records, maintains minutes of meetings, and coordinates administrative communication. Also keeps the club’s Common Seal.

4. Financial Secretary

Manages financial documentation, prepares financial reports, tracks income and expenses, and collaborates with the Treasurer on all monetary matters.

5. Treasurer

Handles the club’s funds, banking, withdrawals, and financial security. Ensures proper accounting and transparency in all transactions.

6. Public Relations Officer (PRO)

Oversees communication with the public, media engagement, announcements, branding, and promotion of club activities.

7. Welfare Officer

Ensures the well‑being of all members and players, handles support needs, and promotes unity and care within the club.

8. Legal Adviser

Provides legal guidance, ensures compliance with national laws and CAC regulations, and protects the club’s interests.

9. Board of Trustees

Serve as the custodians of the club’s legal and structural integrity. They oversee major decisions, property matters, and ensure adherence to the Constitution.
